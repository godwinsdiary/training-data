Thomas Holcroft was one of Godwin's closest friends for many years. He was a sigificant collaborator in Godwin's writings, notably Political Justice as well as being an important dramatist and novelist in his own right. Holcroft left for Germany in 1799, partly due to the notoriety he had gained as an 'acquitted felon' of the 1794 Treason Trials. He returned to England in 1802 and had further success on the stage. Godwin and he fell out over a character in Godwin's Fleetwood which Holcroft felt was based on him. They were reconciled on Holcroft's deathbed.

Where possible, when Godwin refers to Holcrofts plural, we have established his partner based on chronology, as he married several times. Holcroft married in 1765 and has a daughter Ann. His wife died prematurely but no further details are known. In 1774 he married Matilda Tipler, wiith whom he had his son William and daughter Sophy, this wife dying c 1775. He married his third wife, Dinah Robinson, in 1778, with whom he had a daughter Fanny. The diary notes the separation of Holcroft from his third wife on 6 January 1790, and notes her death on 31 October 1790. Godwin seems to have had no independent relationship with Holcroft's third wife (hence, left uncoded). He married his fourth wife, Louisa Mercier on 4 March 1799. The DNB makes no mention of issue from Hocroft's fourth marriage, but Godwin refers to L Ht and sons in the diary from 1803. Also, the account of Holcroft's death in his Life refers to an incident where Holcroft asks his wife if it is her children making a noise on the stair (p. 310). The Morning Chronicle (25 March 1809) reporting Holcroft's death refers to him having left an amiable wife and six children under the age of nine and two daughters from a previous marriage!

There is a reference to a 'Ht' on 4 Jan 1802 which has been left uncoded as Holcroft is still abroad. '2 Hts' and '3 Holcrofts' entries which appear 1802-5 have been coded for Thomas and Louisa. After Holcroft marries Louisa, all instances of 'Hts' have been coded for her as well. Similarly to other entries, she has also been included in any meals Godwin takes at 'Ht's' and for 'Call on Hts' but NOT for any 'call on Ht' or 'Call at Ht's'. N.B. This is despite evidence of Godwin's inconsistent use of the possessive apostrophe ie 'Dine with Nicholsons, Smiths, Ht's, etc' Note also on 9 Aug 1804, Godwin refers to 'L and T Ht' this has been coded as Thomas and Louisa, despite the unusual nature of the reference.

There is a range of references to Holcroft's activities that can be traced through the entries, although the meaning of many of them remains obscure. For example, 17 September 1788 'Explain to Holcroft' and 29 September 1788 'Holcroft explains to Robinson'. In November 1789 a series of entries from 8 November 1789 to 17 November 1789 provides a narrative of the running away of Holcroft's son, their pursuit to Gravesend and then Deal; the suicide of his son, and then his funeral. This is followed in 6 January 1790 by 'Separation of H and sa femme' . Holcroft's third wife (Diana Robinson) dies on 31 October 1790 and an inquest and funeral follow on 2-3 November 1790. Thomas Holcroft married his fourth wife, Louisa Mercier in 1799. She was the daughter of Louis Sebastien Mercier, author of Tableau de Paris (1781-88). The last entry for 'Ht' - on 19 May 1809 is 'MJ to Hts' and refers to a visit to Louisa, n√©e Mercier (Holcroft died 23 March 1809). Also 31 December 1809 is coded for Louisa and also for Fanny Holcroft, but not for Thomas Holcroft (then deceased). The DNB makes no mention of issue from Hocroft's fourth marriage, but Godwin refers to L Ht and sons in the diary from 1803. Also, the account of Holcroft's death in his Life refers to an incident where Holcroft asks his wife if they are her children making a noise on the stair. The Morning Chronicle for 25 March 1809 reported Holcroft's death and referred to him having left an amiable wife and six children under the age of 9 and two daughters from a previous marriage (repeated in Jackson's Oxford Journal).

Louisa remarried on 5 March 1812. The DNB entry for James Kenney, Louisa's second husband, suggests that she came to the marriage with two daughters, as well as Holcroft's two daughters by a previous marriage. It suggests that she and Kenney had four further children. This is irreconcilable, both with the Morning Chronicle's account, and with Godwin's own listing of L Ht and sons. Godwin's diary has their wedding (L Ht marries) at 27 March 1812, whereas the DNB entry is for 5 March. Godwin also refers to L Kenney and sons on 20 July 1813, which suggests continuity with L Ht.

After the wedding, however, L Ht is used to refer to her daughter, also Louisa. This can be seen from entries where L Kenney appears with L Ht (e.g. 20 Feb 1816).

The diary also refers to Harwood Holcroft,, probably one of those sons, named in gratitude after Col. Harwood who was a close friend to Hocroft as well as being his son-in-law (married to Holcroft's daughter Ann)
