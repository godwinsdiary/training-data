1836

18  January  1836 21  January  1836 (V) 14  February  1836 9  March  1836 (V)

  • Name: Kenney, James
  • Gender: Male
  • Birth Date: 1780
  • Death Date: 25  July  1849
  • Occupation: playwright

Husband of Louisa - nee Mercier, previously married to Holcroft. The first entry, 24.August 1811, 'call on Lamb ; adv. L and V Ht and Kenny:', is supported by the presence of Lamb - who was close friends with Kenney according to the DNB and E.V. Lucas, The Life of Charles Lamb - as well as Louisa Holcroft, whom Kenney was to marry on 5.3.1812. 10.11.1813 'Kenney dines; adv. Lbs and H Robinson' is corroborated by Crabb Robinson's diary - 'called and supped with Godwin. The Lambs were there .... Kenney was there' (qu. Lucas, p. 419). This is interesting because it possibly affects our reading of what an adv signifies: it appears that Crabb Robinson had not been invited for supper, happened to call but then did stay for supper. The Kenneys spent time in France, about which the DNB is rather vague - 'after his marriage to Louisa, Kenney lived for several years in St Valéry-sur-Somme'. The Kennys appear in virtually every year of the diary from 1811 and, without evidence of anyone else of the same name or more details of their time in France we have ignored the DNB's suggestion of a continued period living abroad and have assumed they were in France for only a few months at a time or that they moved to France for a more extended period only after Godwin had died. There is evidence of shorter stays in France: Crabb Robinson records meeting them in Paris on 19.8.1822 when they were'just arrived' (qu. Lucas, p. 604), which is at the beginning of an eight month absence from G's diary. Similarly, there is a letter from Lamb to the Kenneys in France dated October 1826 (see William Carew Hazlitt, The Lambs: their lives, their friends, and their correspondence) and the Kenneys disappear from the diary between October 1826 and April 1827. There is a similar six month absence in some other years, suggesting that they were spending some winters in France, perhaps because of Kenney's health (see DNB and Lucas). Other possible inaccuracies in the DNB entry are noted in the entry on Louisa Holcroft. 'L Kenney's' has been lcoded as referring soley to Louisa. . While it is not impossible that James Kenney is present, the fact that there are so few 'L Kenney's' entries compared to a significant number of 'Kenney's' entries (approx 50), suggests that Godwin is distinguishing between the couple and her on her own.
