  • Name: Wollstonecraft, James
  • Gender: Male
  • Birth Date: 1768
  • Death Date: 18  September  1805
  • Occupation: naval officer

See Todd's biography of Wollstonecraft. Mary's brother James Wollstonecraft went to sea, studied at Woolwich in 1788 and then went back to sea; was deported from France; and joined the Royal Navy. He died of yellow fever. Todd gives 1806 as the date of his death but we have entered the details as they appear in the diary, which has 'Jas Wt dies' on 18.9.1805.

'Uncoded references to members of the Wollstonecraft family are: One ref to Edw. Wt in 23.9.1817, unidentified. One ref to F Wt (19.2.1833) unidentified. On the three uncoded Wt jun entries in 1806, see the entry for L Wt

  • William Godwin,Memoirs of the Author of a Vindication of the Rights of Woman, ed. by Pamela Clemit and Gina Luria (Peterborough, Ont.: Broadview Literary Texts, 2001).
