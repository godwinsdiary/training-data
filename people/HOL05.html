  • Name: Holcroft, Louisa (née Mercier)
  • Gender: Female
  • Birth Date:
  • Death Date:

Thomas Holcroft married his fourth wife, Louisa Mercier in 1799. She was the daughter of Louis Sebastien Mercier, author of Tableau de Paris (1781-88). The last entry for 'Ht' - on 19 May 1809 is 'MJ to Hts' and refers to a visit to Louisa, née Mercier (Holcroft died 23 March 1809). Also 31 December 1809 is coded for Louisa and also for Fanny Holcroft, but not for Thomas Holcroft (then deceased). The DNB makes no mention of issue from Hocroft's fourth marriage, but Godwin refers to L Ht and sons in the diary from 1803. Also, the account of Holcroft's death in his Life refers to an incident where Holcroft asks his wife if they are her children making a noise on the stair. The Morning Chronicle for 25 March 1809 reported Holcroft's death and referred to him having left an amiable wife and six children under the age of 9 and two daughters from a previous marriage (repeated in Jackson's Oxford Journal).

Louisa remarried on 5 March 1812. The DNB entry for James Kenney, Louisa's second husband, suggests that she came to the marriage with two daughters, as well as Holcroft's two daughters by a previous marriage. It suggests that she and Kenney had four further children. This is irreconcilable, both with the Morning Chronicle's account, and with Godwin's own listing of L Ht and sons. Godwin's diary has their wedding (L Ht marries) at 27 March 1812, whereas the DNB entry is for 5 March. Godwin also refers to L Kenney and sons on 20 July 1813, which suggests continuity with L Ht.

After the wedding, however, L Ht is used to refer to her daughter, also Louisa. This can be seen from entries where L Kenney appears with L Ht (e.g. 20 Feb 1816).

The diary also refers to Harwood Holcroft,, probably one of those sons, named in gratitude after Col. Harwood who was a close friend to Hocroft as well as being his son-in-law (married to Holcroft's daughter Ann)
