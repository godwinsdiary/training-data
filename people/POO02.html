  • Name: Poole, John
  • Gender: Male
  • Birth Date: 1785
  • Death Date: 4  February  1872
  • Occupation: Playwright

See the letter from John Poole to Godwin confirming arrangements for Godwin to eat a 'bad supper' with him on Thursday 26th. If this went ahead it seems to have been on the 26th of September 1833.

It is clear from Lamb's jounral that he knew John Poole in Paris in 1822 when they both saw a good deal of the Kenneys, and many later connections are linked to the Kenneys. Also, the Poole entries seem to match with Poole's stint in France. Godwin also writes to Poole, and it is difficult to be certain that this is to J Poole, but he makes no effort to identify T Poole, and that suggests that he thinks of the Poole entries after 1810 as the same person.

For a full discussion of the Pooles in the diary see Thomas Poole.

  • MS. Abinger c. 15, fol. 66 : n.d.
  • DNB
  • The Life of Charles Lamb Vol 2, p. 604-5
