Susannah was a prominent member of radical circles in Norwich. She was the wife of John Taylor of Norwich and mother of a number of children who also appear in the diary. She was a celebrated conversationalist and there is an account of her in Janet Ross‚Äôs 1888 Three Generations of Englishwomen.

Instances of 'Susan Taylor' and 'Mrs Taylor' have been identified as Susannah; please see discussion under John Taylor.

Godwin records her death.

  • MS. Abinger c. 12, fols. 98-9: 9 March 1821.

Richard Taylor was a printer who was a fellow of a number of prestigious organizations such as the Linnean Society, the Society of Antiquaries, and the Philological Society. He was the son of John and Susannah Taylor of Norwich who also appear in the diary.

'R Taylor' and 'RT' have been identified as Richard along with some instances of 'Taylor'; see further discusion at John Taylor.
