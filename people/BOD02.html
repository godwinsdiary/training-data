  • Name: Boddington, Samuel
  • Gender: Male
  • Birth Date: 19  June  1766
  • Death Date: 19  April  1843
  • Occupation: West Indies Merchant and Irish Politician

Married to Grace Ashburner, whom he met at George Cadogan Morgan's home; she later eloped with his cousin Benjamin Boddington. After Grace's departure, their children lived for a time with the Morgans.

The Boddington family held large estates in the West Indies and Samuel had been left a fortune by his father who had been a director of the South Sea Company as well as a West India merchant with offices at Mark Lane. After his wife left, Samuel approached his friend, Richard Sharp (politician), a fellow Dissenter, fellow member of the Fishmongers' Company, and both mutual friends of Samuel Rogers, asking if he would join him in business, and eventually a West India company of Boddington, Sharp and Philips (George Philips) was established at 17 Mark Lane. Both Boddington and Philips (later Sir George Philips) followed Sharp's example and became dissenting Whig members of Parliament. Boddington was Member of Parliament (MP) for Tralee from January to May 1807.

There is a question about the relationship between John Boddington and Samuel. Occasions linked to Amelia Alderson have been coded for John; occasions linked to Morgan, Sharp and Rogers have been linked to Samuel.

  • Mary Ann Constantine and Paul Frame (eds) A World of New Ideas: The Travels of George Cadogan Morgan in France and of Richard Price Morgan in America, Cardiff, University of Wales Press, 2012
