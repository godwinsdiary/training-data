1823

14  December  1823

  • Name: Fenwick, John
  • Gender: Male
  • Death Date: 14  December  1823
  • Occupation: reformer

John Fenwick was an Irish radical who had an estranged marriage with Eliza Fenwick, the novelist who also worked in Godwin's Juvenile Library. He may have been a member of the Philomaths, the polite conversation club Godwin was also a member of in the 1790s. Fenwick was a dissolute drinker which contributed to the break-up of his marriage. He was also a friend of Charles Lamb.

'Fenwick' and 'Fenwick's' have been coded as John Fenwick, but there are many references to Mrs Fenwick, and some also to Orlando and Elizabeth Fenwick jnr, their children. There are also references to Thomas Fenwick have been John's brother, who owned a shop in Penzance and provided support and refuge for Mrs Fenwick on a number of occasions. He went bankrupt in 1803 and moved to Portsmouth.

The estrangement between the couple means that two 'Fenwicks' entries may not be to Fenwick and his wife, although there do seem to have been bouts of periodic reconciliation; we have taken 'Mrs and E Fenwick' to refer to the mother and daughter, but it is possible it refers to Eliza Fenwick and her mother in law.

Godwin notes his death.
