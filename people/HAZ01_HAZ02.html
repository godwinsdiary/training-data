  • Name: Hazlitt, William
  • Gender: Male
  • Birth Date: 10  April  1778
  • Death Date: 18  September  1830
  • Occupation: writer
  • Occupation: painter

Hazlitt was a writer with a dissenting background. Reference to the Hazlitt family first appears in the diary in 1794, when Godwin had tea with the 'Hazlitts' - referring to William and his older brother John. In 1794 and 1795 William is taken as being included in references to the 'Hazlits', but not when it is singular - these refer to his painter brother John. It is not likely that they refer to their father, John, who was settled in Shropshire at this stage. There are no references to members of the family in 1796 or 1798, but it is known that in 1797 the essayist was calling on Godwin himself, as he left the only known eyewitness account of Godwin and Wollstonecraft together. So, in 1797, and from 1799 onwards, 'Hazlit' is taken as referring to William, now the primary contact.

Hazlitt and Godwin were very close for a number of years until Hazlitt published a disparaging essay about his dull conversation in The Plain Speaker (1826) which caused a breach.

Godwin recorded his death.

  • Gender: Female
  • Birth Date: 1774
  • Death Date: 1840

Sarah Hazlitt first appears as 'Miss Stodart' in 1806. She married William Hazlitt on 1 May 1808 and is then described as 'Mrs Hazlit' or 'S Hazlit' and included in 'Hazlits' or meals at 'Hazlit's'. They were divorced in 1822 and Hazlitt remarried in April 1824. This makes the identification of 'Mrs W Hazlitt'', who calls on Godwin on 1 June 1824 suspect. The Hazlitt's do not set out on the European tour until the Autumn of 1824. Also, Godwin has fallen into a pattern of referring to Hazlitt's first wife as S Hazlit - which suggests that this may be a call from Mrs Isabella Hazlitt. If so, it is likely the only one: the Hazlitt's separated in Paris the following year.
